OORT CLOUD

The Oort Cloud is a cloud of icy planetismals (big snowballs) that is believed to surround the Sun at distances between 2,000 and 200,000 AU.


WHAT IS THE OORT CLOUD?

  • The Oort Cloud is more than 1,000 times as distant from the Sun as the Kuiper Belt.
  • Objects in the Oort Cloud are at the furthest reaches of the Sun's gravity. Leaving the Oort Cloud would mean leaving the gravitational influence of the Sun and hence entering "interstellar" space (the space between the stars.)
  • We think it's there, though we've never seen any object in the Oort Cloud itself! This is because it is so far away from us, and the objects are spaced so far apart from each other. Space is a big place indeed, so looking for these objects would be like looking for single grains of sand thousands of miles away, and then trying to find another one 10 miles next to it somewhere - a difficult task, to say the least!
