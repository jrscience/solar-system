WELCOME TO THE SOLAR SYSTEM

Welcome home.

Here you can learn about the Solar System. Visit each planet to learn more about it in detail, including whether or not life could exist, or be established, there or on one or more of its moons, explore its facts and figures, and view breath-taking images.

If you're viewing the desktop site, you'll notice your mouse cursor has changed to the Voyager 1 space probe. Since its launch in 1977, Voyager 1 has been travelling through space at a speed of a million miles per day. It has provided us with invaluable information about many of the planets and moons in the Solar System, but it will not enter interstellar space for another... 30,000 YEARS!!

The video below may also help provide some sense of scale. Put simply, the Solar System is incredibly vast!

And yet... it is only one of billions like it in our galaxy. And... our galaxy is one of trillions in the observable universe!!


"The solar system is completely wide open. Almost anywhere we go, I'm sure we would learn a lot."
